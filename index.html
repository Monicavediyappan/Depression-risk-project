The Problem

Why This Project Matters

  • 01 38–62% of people living with HIV/AIDS develop depression or anxiety — yet it goes undiagnosed because symptoms overlap with HIV complications like fatigue and cognitive decline.
  • 02 Untreated depression reduces ART adherence by up to 40%, leading to viral load rebound, CD4 depletion, and accelerated disease progression.
  • 03 Only 27% of PLWH with depression ever receive adequate mental health treatment due to HIV-related stigma forcing patients to hide symptoms.
  • 04 Prior work including the base paper used only 100 patients and 5 survey features — no HIV biomarkers, no deployment, no fairness testing.

Quantum Computing

Two Quantum Approaches

PennyLane VQC for feature selection + Qiskit QSVC for benchmarking — both simulated on CPU, no hardware required.

PennyLane VQC
Variational Quantum Circuit — Feature Selection
0: ─RY─RZ─╭●──────╭X─RY─┤<Z> 1: ─RY─RZ──╰X─╭●──│──RY─┤<Z> 2: ─RY─RZ─────╰X─╭●─RY─┤<Z> 3: ─RY─RZ────────╰X─RY─┤<Z>
  • RY Rotation around Y-axis — encodes patient feature values as quantum angles on the Bloch sphere
  • RZ Rotation around Z-axis — adds phase encoding for second layer of feature information
  • CNOT Controlled-NOT — creates quantum entanglement between adjacent qubits, capturing feature correlations
  • PauliZ Measurement — collapses quantum state to classical output, producing feature importance score
Qiskit QSVC
Quantum Kernel SVC — Base Paper Benchmark
ZZFeatureMap (same as base paper): feature_dim = 2 reps = 1 entanglement = linear Kernel: FidelityQuantumKernel Classifier: QSVC Standalone result: ~52% accuracy (matches base paper finding) Inside stacking ensemble: 0.98 AUC (quantum stacking fixes weakness!)
  • QSVC Quantum Support Vector Classifier — same architecture as base paper for direct comparison
  • ZZMap ZZFeatureMap maps classical data into quantum Hilbert space for kernel similarity
  • Result Standalone QSVC = 52% accuracy. Inside stacking = 0.98 AUC. Proves quantum stacking works.
  • ECE Demonstrated quantum noise simulation, Bloch sphere, Bell state, interference effects
Explainability

Triple XAI Suite

Three independent, complementary explainability methods — each answering a different clinical question. The base paper used only one.

SHAP
SHapley Additive exPlanations
"Which features matter most globally?"
TreeExplainer on the RF base model inside the stacking ensemble. 500 test patients sampled. Produces beeswarm plots, bar charts, and patient-level waterfall plots.
Top finding: PHQ9_Score, GAD7_Score,
Social_Support drive highest risk.
CD4_Count, ART_Adherence top HIV markers.
LIME
Local Interpretable Model-Agnostic Explanations
"Why was THIS patient flagged?"
LimeTabularExplainer with 1000 perturbation samples. Explains individual patients locally — not global averages. Side-by-side SHAP vs LIME comparison validates consistency.
Both SHAP and LIME agree on top 5
risk factors for same patient —
validating explanation reliability.
DiCE
Diverse Counterfactual Explanations
"What needs to change to reduce risk?"
Generates 3 diverse counterfactuals showing minimum feature changes to flip prediction from High Risk to Low Risk. Clinically actionable intervention targets.
Example: "If Social_Support 2→6
and Sleep_Quality 3→7, patient
flips from High Risk to Low Risk."
